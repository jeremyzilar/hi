# High Ground Model

Details from the High Ground conference that Juliette runs in Santa Fe each year. This serves as the primary inspiration for our gathering format.

## Core Format

### Presentation Structure

- **Talk Duration**: 7 minutes
- **Discussion Duration**: 20 minutes per talk
- **Total Time per Conversation**: ~27 minutes
- **Presentation Type**: Called "conversations" (not "talks" or "presentations")

### Presentation Rules

- **No slides** - Pure conversation
- **No predetermined order** - Schedule assembles on-site
- **No fixed schedule** - One person curates segments on the spot
- **Universal Participation** - Everyone who participates has to present

### Content Guidelines

Each person is asked to bring something to talk about, with specific constraints:

**Purpose of talks:**
- Generate discussion
- Talk about what's going on in the world
- Share perspectives and observations

**NOT the purpose:**
- Not to show off
- Not to sell something
- Not to toot horns
- No personal motivation or self-promotion

## Social Dynamics

### Why This Works

The format is possible because:
- Folks know each other over many years
- This is a bunch of friends talking together
- Jokes and language are all in common
- Established trust and shared context

### The Origin

- Started because a bunch of old people want to stay clued in to understanding what's going on
- Functions as a pulse check on what is happening in Design and its relationship to the world
- Built on existing friendships and professional relationships

## Selection & Participation

### How People Are Chosen

- **Curated selection** - People are selected to attend
- **Limited capacity** - There is a limited amount of time
- **Intentional curation** - New people are selected based on what the organizer wants to hear about
- **Not equitable by design** - "Other things try to be equitable, this is not that"

### The Network Model

- More of a friends network that shares
- To include someone new, someone has to step down
- Maintains consistent group size and dynamic
- Values depth of existing relationships

## Key Principles

1. **Trust-based** - Assumes good faith and shared values
2. **Discussion-focused** - The talk is a starting point, not the end goal
3. **Spontaneous** - No rigid schedule allows for organic flow
4. **Curated** - Intentional about who participates and why
5. **Non-promotional** - Pure exploration and conversation
6. **Friend-first** - Built on existing relationships and trust

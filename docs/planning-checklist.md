# Planning Checklist

Action items and next steps for making this gathering happen.

## Phase 1: Core Decisions (Do First)

### Fundamental Questions
- [ ] What is the primary goal? (pulse check, connection, specific topic?)
- [ ] Who is the intended audience? (list potential attendees)
- [ ] What is your role? (organizer, curator, participant?)
- [ ] What is the ideal size? (recommend: 15-18 people)
- [ ] What is the ideal duration? (recommend: 3 nights/4 days)
- [ ] What is the timeline? (target dates, season considerations)
- [ ] What is the budget reality? (per-person max, total budget cap)

### Key Decision
- [ ] **Decision**: Commit to approximate size, duration, and dates
  - Example: "15-18 people, 3 nights, October 2026"

## Phase 2: Venue Confirmation

### Contact Ghost Ranch
- [ ] Check availability for target dates
- [ ] Confirm pricing (request 2026 rates if not already confirmed)
- [ ] Ask about multi-day discounts
- [ ] Clarify cancellation policy
- [ ] Reserve preferred meeting space
- [ ] Ask follow-up questions from [ghost-ranch-info.md](./ghost-ranch-info.md)

### Venue Details to Confirm
- [ ] Specific room/space reservation
- [ ] Lodging block (how many rooms, what types)
- [ ] Meal arrangements (all meals vs. selective)
- [ ] Payment schedule and deposit
- [ ] Contract terms
- [ ] Insurance requirements
- [ ] What happens if attendance changes

### Venue Selection
- [ ] **Decision**: Book specific dates and spaces at Ghost Ranch
- [ ] Pay deposit
- [ ] Get confirmation in writing

## Phase 3: Attendee Planning

### The Core Group
- [ ] List potential attendees (start with 20-25 names for 15-18 target)
- [ ] Consider: Who do you want to hear from?
- [ ] Consider: Who brings needed perspectives?
- [ ] Consider: Who knows each other already?
- [ ] Consider: Who are the newcomers?

### Invitation Strategy
- [ ] Draft invitation language
- [ ] Clarify expectations (everyone presents, format, etc.)
- [ ] Set RSVP deadline
- [ ] Communicate costs clearly
- [ ] Explain the High Ground model and purpose
- [ ] Clarify this is curated/invitation-only

### Attendee Management
- [ ] Send invitations (in waves or all at once?)
- [ ] Track RSVPs
- [ ] Manage waitlist if needed
- [ ] Collect deposits if requiring commitment
- [ ] Send pre-event communications

## Phase 4: Format & Structure Design

### Adapt High Ground Model
- [ ] Decide on presentation timing (keep 7min + 20min?)
- [ ] Determine curator approach (one person? rotating?)
- [ ] Plan for topic collection (how do people submit ideas?)
- [ ] Design schedule assembly process
- [ ] Plan opening and closing sessions
- [ ] Balance structured vs. unstructured time

### Daily Rhythm
- [ ] Design typical daily schedule
- [ ] Plan meal times around conversations
- [ ] Build in break/reflection time
- [ ] Consider outdoor/activity options
- [ ] Plan for evening social time

### Ground Rules
- [ ] Document participation expectations
- [ ] Set technology/device policy
- [ ] Clarify confidentiality/sharing norms
- [ ] Address photography/documentation approach
- [ ] Define what "no self-promotion" means

## Phase 5: Logistics Planning

### Travel & Transportation
- [ ] Share travel information with attendees
- [ ] Coordinate airport pickups if needed
- [ ] Organize carpools for venues with limited parking
- [ ] Provide directions and parking info
- [ ] Set arrival/departure times

### On-Site Logistics
- [ ] Finalize room assignments
- [ ] Create name tags or welcome materials
- [ ] Prepare conversation topic collection method
- [ ] Test A/V if using any
- [ ] Plan for dietary restrictions
- [ ] Arrange any special requests

### Materials & Supplies
- [ ] Name tags/place cards
- [ ] Method for collecting conversation topics (cards? board?)
- [ ] Timer for talks
- [ ] Any handouts or printed materials
- [ ] Welcome packet/schedule overview
- [ ] Snacks/beverages for meeting space (if allowed)

## Phase 6: Pre-Event Preparation

### Communications (2-3 months out)
- [ ] Send "save the date" to potential attendees
- [ ] Share purpose, format, and expectations

### Communications (1 month out)
- [ ] Send detailed logistics email
- [ ] Request conversation topics
- [ ] Share packing list and what to expect
- [ ] Provide Ghost Ranch information
- [ ] Collect dietary restrictions
- [ ] Finalize headcount with venue

### Communications (1 week out)
- [ ] Send final details and schedule
- [ ] Share attendee list (if appropriate)
- [ ] Confirm arrival times
- [ ] Share emergency contact info
- [ ] Weather forecast and packing tips

### Curator Preparation
- [ ] Review all submitted conversation topics
- [ ] Develop sense of themes/threads
- [ ] Prepare opening remarks
- [ ] Plan for energy management across days
- [ ] Have backup plans for scheduling

## Phase 7: During Event

### Day 1 (Arrival)
- [ ] Welcome attendees as they arrive
- [ ] Facilitate informal introductions
- [ ] Host opening dinner
- [ ] Frame the gathering and expectations
- [ ] Collect/review conversation topics
- [ ] Set tone for intimacy and trust

### Days 2-3 (Core Program)
- [ ] Facilitate conversation sessions
- [ ] Maintain timing (7min talk + 20min discussion)
- [ ] Read the room and adjust flow
- [ ] Ensure everyone presents
- [ ] Protect meal/break times
- [ ] Foster informal connections

### Day 4 (Departure)
- [ ] Final conversation session
- [ ] Closing reflection/circle
- [ ] Discuss potential for future gatherings
- [ ] Collect feedback
- [ ] Coordinate departures

## Phase 8: Post-Event

### Immediate Follow-Up (within 1 week)
- [ ] Send thank you to attendees
- [ ] Share any photos (if taken)
- [ ] Collect feedback via survey or conversation
- [ ] Document what worked and what didn't
- [ ] Thank Ghost Ranch and provide feedback

### Longer-Term (within 1 month)
- [ ] Compile feedback and learnings
- [ ] Decide: Do we do this again?
- [ ] If yes: When and what changes?
- [ ] Maintain connection with attendees
- [ ] Archive materials and notes

### Reflection Questions
- [ ] Did we achieve our purpose?
- [ ] Was the size right?
- [ ] Was the duration right?
- [ ] Did the High Ground format work?
- [ ] What would we change?
- [ ] Who should return? Who should be invited next time?
- [ ] Is Ghost Ranch the right venue?

## Budget Tracking

### Revenue (if splitting costs)
- [ ] Calculate per-person cost
- [ ] Determine payment method
- [ ] Set payment deadline
- [ ] Track payments received
- [ ] Handle any subsidies or scholarships

### Expenses
- [ ] Ghost Ranch deposit: $______
- [ ] Ghost Ranch final payment: $______
- [ ] Meeting space rental: $______
- [ ] Meals: $______
- [ ] Materials/supplies: $______
- [ ] Any other costs: $______
- [ ] **Total**: $______

## Questions to Revisit Regularly

1. Are we staying true to the High Ground model?
2. Are we maintaining intimacy and trust?
3. Is the curator role working?
4. Are conversations generating real discussion?
5. Is anyone feeling left out or unheard?
6. Is the pace sustainable?
7. Are we achieving the "pulse check" goal?

## Critical Success Factors

✅ Right people in the room  
✅ Clear expectations set upfront  
✅ Trust and safety established  
✅ Conversations generate genuine discussion  
✅ Spontaneity and organic flow maintained  
✅ Everyone feels heard and valued  
✅ Worth the time and financial investment  
✅ People want to do it again

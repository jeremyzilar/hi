# Purpose & Values

What we want to get out of this gathering and the values that guide it.

## Primary Purpose

Drawing from High Ground's origin:

> "The whole thing kicked off because a bunch of old people want to stay clued in to understanding what is going on. It is a pulse check on what is happening in Design and it's relationship to the world."

### Our Purpose

- **Stay clued in** - Maintain connection to what's happening
- **Pulse check** - Regular temperature reading on design/culture/world
- **Generate discussion** - The conversation is the point
- **Deepen connections** - Strengthen existing relationships
- **Share perspectives** - Learn from each other's unique vantage points

## Core Values

### 1. Trust & Friendship First

- Built on existing relationships
- "A bunch of friends talking together"
- Shared language and context
- Good faith assumed

### 2. Discussion Over Performance

- Talks exist to generate conversation
- Not about showing off
- Not about selling or self-promotion
- The 20-minute discussion is more important than the 7-minute talk

### 3. Intentional Curation

- "Other things try to be equitable, this is not that"
- More of a friends network that shares
- People selected based on what organizer wants to hear about
- Quality over quantity

### 4. Spontaneity & Flow

- No predetermined schedule
- Curator reads the room
- Let conversations emerge organically
- Trust the process

### 5. Substance & Relevance

- Talk about what's going on in the world
- Focus on meaningful observations
- Bring perspective and insight
- No filler, no fluff

## What This Is NOT

- ❌ A traditional conference
- ❌ A networking event
- ❌ A showcase or portfolio review
- ❌ A sales opportunity
- ❌ An equitable or democratic process
- ❌ Open to anyone who wants to attend

## What This IS

- ✅ A gathering of friends
- ✅ A space for real conversation
- ✅ A pulse check on the world
- ✅ An intentional, curated group
- ✅ A place to think together
- ✅ A way to stay connected and relevant

## The "Over The Horizon" Framing

Potential themes to explore:

- What's coming that we need to pay attention to?
- What are we noticing at the edges?
- What patterns are emerging?
- What questions are we sitting with?
- What's changing that others might not see yet?

## Success Metrics

How do we know this worked?

- Did we have conversations we couldn't have elsewhere?
- Do we leave with new perspectives or insights?
- Do we feel more connected to the group?
- Do we feel more clued in to what's happening?
- Did the discussions generate new thinking?
- Would we want to do this again?

## Who Should Be There

### Characteristics
- Thoughtful observers of culture/design/world
- Good conversationalists
- Willing to be vulnerable and real
- Not there to sell or promote
- Trusted by the group
- Have something genuine to share

### The Selection Question
When considering someone new: "What do we want to hear from this person? What perspective do they bring that we need?"

## Outputs (or lack thereof)

### What We Don't Need
- No proceedings or published talks
- No social media required
- No external-facing content

### What Might Be Useful
- Private shared notes?
- Reading list or resources shared?
- Photos for memory (not promotion)?
- Simple way to stay connected between gatherings?

## Evolution Over Time

This gathering could:
- Become an annual rhythm
- Stay small and intimate
- Slowly evolve membership as people's lives change
- Develop its own traditions and norms
- Remain responsive to what the group needs

The key is keeping the core purpose and values intact while allowing natural evolution.

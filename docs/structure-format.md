# Structure & Format

How the gathering would be organized, adapting the High Ground model to a multi-day format at Ghost Ranch.

## Base Format (from High Ground)

- 7-minute talks + 20-minute discussions
- No slides, no predetermined schedule
- On-site curation and assembly
- Everyone presents
- See [High Ground Model](./high-ground-model.md) for full details

## Adapting to Multi-Day Format

### Schedule Framework

#### Option A: Intensive Days
- **Morning session**: 3-4 conversations (9am - 12:30pm)
- **Lunch + break**: (12:30pm - 2:30pm)
- **Afternoon session**: 3-4 conversations (2:30pm - 5:30pm)
- **Evening**: Unstructured social time, dinner

**Pros**: Lots of content, keeps energy high  
**Cons**: Can be exhausting, less time for organic connection

#### Option B: Relaxed Pace
- **Morning session**: 2-3 conversations (10am - 12pm)
- **Lunch + extended break**: (12pm - 3pm)
- **Afternoon session**: 2-3 conversations (3pm - 5pm)
- **Evening**: Unstructured time, dinner, optional activities

**Pros**: More breathing room, time for side conversations  
**Cons**: Fewer total conversations, need more days

#### Option C: Variable Rhythm
- Different pacing each day
- Day 1: Light (arrive, settle, evening intro)
- Day 2-3: Full intensity
- Day 4: Light (wrap-up, depart)

### Duration Options

#### 2-Day Weekend
- Arrive Friday evening
- Full Saturday + Sunday morning
- Depart Sunday afternoon
- **Estimate**: 8-12 conversations total

#### 3-Day Extended
- Arrive Thursday evening
- Full Friday, Saturday, Sunday morning
- Depart Sunday afternoon
- **Estimate**: 15-20 conversations total

#### 4-Day Retreat
- Arrive Wednesday evening
- Full Thursday, Friday, Saturday
- Depart Sunday morning
- **Estimate**: 20-25 conversations total

## Group Size Considerations

### Small Group (8-12 people)
**Pros:**
- Very intimate
- Everyone truly heard
- Easier to schedule organically
- Lower total cost

**Cons:**
- Less diversity of perspectives
- Fewer total conversations
- Higher per-person cost likely

### Medium Group (15-20 people)
**Pros:**
- Good balance of intimacy and diversity
- Rich pool of conversations
- Can handle some absences/scheduling

**Cons:**
- Harder to ensure everyone connects deeply
- More complex dynamics

### Large Group (25-30 people)
**Pros:**
- Maximum diversity of perspectives
- Lots of conversations to choose from

**Cons:**
- May lose intimacy
- Harder to maintain "friends talking" vibe
- Requires more structure

## On-Site Curation

### Curator Role

The curator (based on High Ground model):
- Assembles segments on the spot
- Reads the room
- Decides what conversation happens next
- Manages time and flow

## Supporting Activities

### Optional Unstructured Time

Beyond formal conversations:
- Group meals (natural conversation)
- Hikes or outdoor activities
- Evening campfires or social time
- Optional breakout discussions
- Free time for rest/reflection

### Opening & Closing

**Opening** (First evening):
- Arrivals and settling
- Dinner
- Light introduction/framing
- Set expectations
- Share topics people brought

**Closing** (Final morning):
- Final conversation session
- Reflection/synthesis
- Closing circle
- Logistics for staying connected

## Physical Space Considerations

### Main Conversation Space

Requirements:
- Seating in a circle or around a table
- Good acoustics
- Natural light if possible
- Comfortable for 3-4 hours at a time
- No distractions

### Supporting Spaces

Nice to have:
- Breakout areas for side conversations
- Quiet spaces for reflection/prep
- Outdoor gathering spaces
- Walking paths
